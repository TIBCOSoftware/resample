Difference between Two Means: t Test, Resample

The two-sample t-test is used to test whether two samples come from distributions with the same means. The samples are assumed to come from Gaussian (normal) distributions. If this is not the case, then permutation tests or a nonparametric test, such as the Wilcoxon rank sum test, may be more appropriate for testing, and bootstrapping more appropriate for confidence intervals.

This test handles both paired and independent samples.

The resampling methods support stratified sampling.
